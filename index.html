Mapping the Information Ecology of Homeownership Pathways: A Human-Centered Approach

Making homeownership accessible and achievable for everyone, especially people with physical disabilities, through intelligent, personalized guidance.

Our Mission

Navigating the path to homeownership can be overwhelming. Programs, resources, and policies vary dramatically across states and counties, and information is scattered across hundreds of websites and organizations. For many people, especially those with disabilities, low incomes, or unique circumstances, finding the right help feels impossible.

We're building an information ecology-informed, AI-based information system that changes this. By combining comprehensive data collection, human-centered design, and AI technology, we're creating a platform that provides personalized guidance tailored to each person's unique situation, such as their location, income, legal status, disability status, credit history, and more.

Our approach brings together large-scale surveys and ecology-aware data collection pipelines to identify credible resources, expert interviews to understand real needs and challenges, and AI-powered recommendations refined through continuous human feedback. Our approach combines expertise in theories of information access, human-in-the-loop techniques, customized AI models, and community-based research in disabilities to deliver a stand-alone system that connects people across the United States with the resources, programs, and knowledge they need to achieve homeownership.
